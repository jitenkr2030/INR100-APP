## Digital Payment Systems Fundamentals

### What are Digital Payments?
Digital payments refer to electronic transactions that allow money to be sent, received, or stored using digital devices without the use of physical currency. They include various technologies and platforms that facilitate electronic financial transactions.

### Evolution of Payments

#### Traditional Payments
- **Cash**: Physical currency exchange
- **Checks**: Paper-based payment orders
- **Bank**: Bank transfers and drafts
- **Cards**: Magnetic stripe cards
- **Limits**: Geographic and time limitations

#### Digital Transformation
- **Cards**: EMV chip cards and contactless
- **Online**: Online banking and e-commerce
- **Mobile**: Mobile banking applications
- **Wallets**: Digital wallets and payment apps
- **Real-time**: Real-time payment systems

### Payment System Components

#### Payment Infrastructure
- **Banks**: Banking network infrastructure

- **Voice**: Voice-activated payments

## Major Digital Payment Technologies

### Unified Payments Interface (UPI)

#### What is UPI?
UPI is a real-time payment system developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) that enables instant money transfers between bank accounts using mobile devices.

#### Key Features
- **Instant**: Real-time money transfers
- **Simple**: Simple payment interface
- **Secure**: Bank-grade security
- **Interoperable**: Works across all banks
- **Mobile**: Mobile-first design

#### UPI Ecosystem
- **Banks**: 300+ banks participating
- **Apps**: Multiple UPI-enabled apps
- **Merchants**: Millions of merchants accepting UPI
- **Users**: 200+ million active users
- **Volume**: 10+ billion monthly transactions

#### Popular UPI Apps
- **PhonePe**: Walmart-backed UPI platform
- **Google Pay**: Google's UPI solution
- **Paytm**: Paytm's UPI offering
- **Amazon**: Amazon Pay UPI
- **BHIM**: Government UPI app
