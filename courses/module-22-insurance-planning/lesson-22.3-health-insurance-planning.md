## Health Insurance Fundamentals

### What is Health Insurance?
Health insurance is a contract between an individual and an insurance company where the insurer agrees to pay for medical expenses in exchange for regular premium payments. It provides financial protection against high medical costs and ensures access to quality healthcare.

### Key Features

#### Coverage Scope
- **Medical**: Hospitalization and medical expenses
- **Preventive**: Preventive care and check-ups
- **Emergency**: Emergency medical services
- **Surgery**: Surgical procedures and treatments
- **Medicines**: Prescription medications

#### Financial Protection
- **Cashless**: Cashless treatment at network hospitals
- **Reimbursement**: Reimbursement for out-of-pocket expenses
- **Coverage**: Comprehensive coverage options
- **Limits**: Coverage limits and sub-limits
- **Exclusions**: Specific exclusions and limitations

### Types of Health Insurance

#### Individual Health Insurance
- **Personal**: Coverage for individual policyholder
- **Customizable**: Customizable coverage options
- **Personal**: Personal coverage benefits
- **Flexible**: Flexible sum insured options
- **Add-ons**: Additional coverage riders

#### Family Floater
- **Family**: Single policy for entire family
- **Shared**: Shared sum insured among family members
- **Cost-Effective**: More cost-effective for families
- **Flexibility**: Flexible coverage options
- **Renewal**: Family renewal options

#### Group Health Insurance
- **Employer**: Provided by employer
- **Automatic**: Automatic coverage for employees
- **Cost**: Lower cost than individual policies
- **Limited**: Limited customization options
- **Portability**: Portability issues when changing jobs
