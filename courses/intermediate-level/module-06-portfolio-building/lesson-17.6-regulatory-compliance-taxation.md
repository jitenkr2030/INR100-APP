## Cryptocurrency Taxation Fundamentals

### Tax Treatment Principles

#### Property vs Currency Debate
- **IRS Position**: Cryptocurrencies treated as property in US
- **Global Variations**: Different approaches across jurisdictions
- **Implications**: Affects capital gains vs ordinary income treatment
- **Reporting**: Different reporting requirements

#### Capital Gains vs Ordinary Income
- **Capital Gains**: Long-term holdings (typically >1 year)
- **Ordinary Income**: Short-term holdings, mining, staking
- **Tax Rates**: Different rates for capital gains vs ordinary income
- **Holding Period**: Importance of tracking acquisition dates

#### Realization Events
- **Sales**: Selling cryptocurrency for fiat currency
- **Exchanges**: Trading one cryptocurrency for another
- **Payments**: Using cryptocurrency for goods or services
- **Transfers**: Moving between wallets (generally not taxable)

### Common Taxable Events

#### Cryptocurrency Sales
- **Fiat Sales**: Selling crypto for US dollars or other fiat
- **Price Calculation**: Sale price minus basis (cost)
- **Capital Gain/Loss**: Difference between sale price and cost basis
- **Wash Sale Rules**: Generally not applicable to cryptocurrency

#### Cryptocurrency Exchanges
- **Like-Kind Exchange**: No longer available for cryptocurrency
- **Taxable Event**: Each exchange is a taxable event
- **Basis Calculation**: New basis equals old basis
- **Holding Period**: Carry over holding period to new cryptocurrency

#### Cryptocurrency Payments
- **Goods/Services**: Using crypto to purchase goods or services
- **Income**: Amount received is ordinary income
- **Self-Employment**: May trigger self-employment tax
- **Business Use**: Different rules for business vs personal use

#### Mining and Staking
- **Income at Receipt**: Income recognized when received
- **Fair Market Value**: Value at time of receipt
- **Mining Expenses**: Deductible business expenses
- **Equipment Depreciation**: Capital equipment depreciation

#### DeFi Transactions
- **Yield Farming**: Income from rewards and fees
- **Liquidity Provision**: Income from trading fees
- **Interest Income**: Interest from lending protocols
- **Complicated Tracking**: May require professional help

### Tax Calculation Methods

#### FIFO (First In, First Out)
- **Method**: Sell oldest purchased coins first
- **Simplicity**: Easiest method to calculate
- **IRS Requirement**: Default method if not specified
- **Documentation**: Requires detailed purchase records

#### LIFO (Last In, First Out)
- **Method**: Sell most recently purchased coins first
- **Tax Advantage**: May result in lower taxes in rising markets
- **Documentation**: Requires detailed record keeping
- **Consistency**: Must use consistently year to year

#### Specific Identification
- **Method**: Identify specific coins to sell
- **Tax Optimization**: Can optimize for lowest tax liability
- **Requirements**: Must be able to identify specific coins
- **Records**: Detailed records required

#### Average Cost Method
- **Method**: Average cost of all purchases
- **Simplicity**: Easier than specific identification
- **Limited Use**: Not available for all cryptocurrencies
- **Documentation**: Still requires purchase records

## Indian Cryptocurrency Tax Framework

### Current Tax Rules (as of 2023)

#### Income Tax on Cryptocurrency
- **Tax Rate**: 30% flat rate on cryptocurrency gains
- **No Deductions**: No deductions allowed except cost of acquisition
- **No Losses**: No offsetting of losses against other income
- **Surcharge**: Additional surcharge for high-income earners

#### GST Implications
- **Services**: Cryptocurrency exchange services subject to 18% GST
- **Goods**: Debate over whether cryptocurrency is goods or service
- **Input Tax Credit**: Limited input tax credit availability
- **Compliance**: Complex compliance requirements

#### TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)
- **Threshold**: TDS applicable above certain transaction limits
- **Rate**: 1% TDS on cryptocurrency transactions
- **Credits**: TDS can be claimed as credit against tax liability
- **Documentation**: Proper documentation required

### Record Keeping Requirements

#### Transaction Records
- **Date/Time**: Exact date and time of transaction
- **Amount**: Amount of cryptocurrency transacted
- **Price**: Price in Indian Rupees at time of transaction
- **Fees**: Transaction fees paid
- **Wallet Addresses**: Sending and receiving wallet addresses

#### Cost Basis Records
- **Purchase Price**: Original purchase price in INR
- **Exchange Rate**: Exchange rate at time of purchase
- **Transaction Fees**: Fees paid during purchase
- **Total Cost**: Total cost including fees

#### Income Records
- **Mining Income**: Income from cryptocurrency mining
- **Staking Rewards**: Income from staking rewards
- **Yield Farming**: Income from DeFi activities
- **Fair Market Value**: Value in INR at time of receipt

### Compliance Procedures

#### Annual Tax Filing
- **ITR Form**: File appropriate ITR form (ITR-3 for business income)
- **Schedule**: Include cryptocurrency transactions in schedule
- **Documentation**: Maintain supporting documentation
- **Professional Help**: Consider professional assistance

#### Quarterly Returns
- **TDS Returns**: File quarterly TDS returns
- **Form 24Q**: TDS on salaries (if applicable)
- **Form 26Q**: TDS on securities (for crypto exchanges)
- **Form 27Q**: TDS on interest/dividends
