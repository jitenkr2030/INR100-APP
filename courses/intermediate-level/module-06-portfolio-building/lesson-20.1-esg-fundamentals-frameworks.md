## ESG Investment Fundamentals

### What is ESG Investing?
ESG investing considers Environmental, Social, and Governance factors alongside traditional financial metrics to make investment decisions. It represents a shift from purely profit-focused investing to sustainable and responsible investing.

### The Three Pillars of ESG

#### Environmental (E)
- **Climate Change**: Carbon footprint and greenhouse gas emissions
- **Resource Use**: Water usage, waste management, energy efficiency
- **Pollution**: Air, water, and soil pollution control
- **Biodiversity**: Impact on ecosystems and biodiversity
- **Renewable Energy**: Transition to clean energy sources

#### Social (S)
- **Human Rights**: Labor practices, human rights protection
- **Employee Relations**: Workplace safety, fair wages, diversity
- **Community Relations**: Local community impact and engagement
- **Product Safety**: Consumer protection and product responsibility
- **Supply Chain**: Ethical sourcing and supply chain management

#### Governance (G)
- **Board Structure**: Board independence, diversity, expertise
- **Executive Compensation**: Fair and transparent compensation
- **Shareholder Rights**: Minority shareholder protection
- **Ethics**: Business ethics and anti-corruption measures
- **Transparency**: Financial reporting and disclosure quality

### ESG vs Traditional Investing

| Aspect | Traditional Investing | ESG Investing |
|--------|----------------------|---------------|
| **Focus** | Financial returns only | Financial + sustainability |
| **Metrics** | Revenue, profit, ROE | + Environmental, social, governance |
| **Time Horizon** | Short to medium term | Long-term sustainability focus |
| **Risk Assessment** | Financial risk | + ESG risk factors |
| **Stakeholders** | Shareholders | All stakeholders (employees, community, environment) |

## ESG Rating Agencies and Frameworks

### Major ESG Rating Agencies

#### MSCI ESG Ratings
- **Scale**: AAA to CCC scale
- **Methodology**: Industry-specific weighting
- **Coverage**: 8,500+ companies globally
- **Focus**: Material ESG issues by industry
- **Data**: Extensive company engagement

**MSCI ESG Key Features:**
- **Industry Normalized**: Scores relative to industry peers
- **Materiality Map**: Industry-specific material issues
- **controversies**: Company controversy tracking
- **Leadership**: ESG leaders and laggards identification

#### Sustainalytics
- **Risk Scores**: ESG risk scores from 0-100
- **Risk Categories**: Exposure and management scores
- **Controversies**: Controversy research and analysis
- **Global Reach**: 15,000+ companies covered
- **Integration**: Integration with major index providers

**Sustainalytics Framework:**
- **Corporate Governance**: 20% weight
- **Human Capital**: 20% weight
- **Product Governance**: 20% weight
- **Carbon**: 15% weight
- **Resource Use**: 10% weight
- **Other**: 15% weight

#### Refinitiv (formerly Thomson Reuters)
- **ESG Scores**: Environmental, Social, Governance scores
- **Percentile Rankings**: Percentile rankings vs peers
- **Data Points**: 400+ ESG data points
- **Disclosure**: Company disclosure quality assessment
- **Historical**: Historical trend analysis

#### S&P Global ESG Scores
- **Environmental**: Environmental pillar score
- **Social**: Social pillar score
- **Governance**: Governance pillar score
- **Combined**: Weighted combined ESG score
- **Peers**: Peer comparison and benchmarking

### ESG Framework Standards

#### UN Global Compact Principles
- **Human Rights**: 1-2 principles
- **Labour**: 3-6 principles
- **Environment**: 7-9 principles
- **Anti-Corruption**: 10th principle
- **Reporting**: Annual communication on progress

#### GRI (Global Reporting Initiative)
- **Universal Standards**: Core reporting standards
- **Economic**: Economic performance and impacts
- **Environmental**: Environmental performance
- **Social**: Social performance including labor
- **Sector**: Industry-specific standards

#### SASB (Sustainability Accounting Standards Board)
- **Industry Standards**: Industry-specific standards
- **Materiality**: Financially material ESG issues
- **Investors**: Designed for investor decision-making
- **Comparison**: Standardized comparison across companies
- **Integration**: Integration with financial reporting

#### TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures)
- **Governance**: Climate-related governance
- **Strategy**: Climate-related risks and opportunities
- **Risk Management**: Climate-related risk management
- **Metrics**: Climate-related metrics and targets
- **Scenario**: Climate scenario analysis

## ESG Investment Strategies

### ESG Integration Strategy
- **Financial + ESG**: Integration of ESG factors in financial analysis
- **Risk Management**: ESG risk assessment and management
- **Opportunity**: ESG opportunities identification
- **Active Ownership**: Engagement with portfolio companies
- **Long-term**: Long-term value creation focus

### Negative Screening Strategy
- **Exclusions**: Exclude companies/sectors based on ESG criteria
- **Sin Stocks**: Exclude tobacco, weapons, gambling
- **Environmental**: Exclude high carbon emitters
- **Social**: Exclude companies with poor labor practices
- **Governance**: Exclude companies with poor governance

### Positive Screening Strategy
- **Best-in-Class**: Select ESG leaders within each sector
- **Thresholds**: Meet minimum ESG score requirements
- **Leaders**: Focus on ESG leaders and improvers
- **Sector**: Sector-specific ESG criteria
- **Peer Comparison**: Compare against industry peers

### Thematic Investing
- **Climate Change**: Climate-focused investments
- **Clean Energy**: Renewable energy investments
- **Water**: Water scarcity solutions
- **Health**: Healthcare and wellness
- **Education**: Education and skills development

### Impact Investing
- **Measurable Impact**: Measurable positive impact
- **Financial Return**: Target financial return
- **ESG Solutions**: Investments in ESG solutions
- **Social Impact**: Measurable social outcomes
- **Reporting**: Impact measurement and reporting

## ESG Challenges and Limitations

### Data Challenges

#### Data Quality
- **Inconsistent**: Inconsistent ESG data quality
- **Gaps**: Significant data gaps
- **Standardization**: Lack of standardization
- **Verification**: Limited third-party verification
- **Subjectivity**: Subjective scoring methodologies

#### Data Availability
- **Coverage**: Limited coverage, especially small companies
- **Frequency**: Infrequent data updates
- **Granularity**: Limited data granularity
- **Historical**: Limited historical data
- **Access**: Data access and cost issues

### Rating Divergence

#### Methodology Differences
- **Weighting**: Different factor weightings
- **Focus**: Different ESG factor focus
- **Scale**: Different rating scales
- **Time**: Different time horizons
- **Stakeholder**: Different stakeholder focus

#### Correlation Issues
- **Low Correlation**: Low correlation between ratings
- **Inconsistent**: Inconsistent ratings for same company
- **Industry Bias**: Industry bias in methodologies
- **Regional**: Regional bias in ratings
- **Size Bias**: Bias toward larger companies

### Greenwashing

#### Definition and Examples
- **Greenwashing**: Misleading ESG claims
- **Marketing**: Marketing over substance
- **Selective**: Selective disclosure of ESG information
- **Vague**: Vague ESG commitments
- **Impact**: Disconnect between claims and impact

#### Detection and Prevention
- **Verification**: Independent verification
- **Standards**: Use of recognized standards
- **Data**: Multiple data sources
- **Engagement**: Direct company engagement
- **Transparency**: Transparent methodology disclosure
