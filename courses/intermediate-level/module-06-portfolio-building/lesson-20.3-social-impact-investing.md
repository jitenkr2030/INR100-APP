## Social Impact Investing Fundamentals

### What is Social Impact Investing?
Social impact investing involves making investments with the intention to generate positive, measurable social and environmental impact alongside financial returns. It goes beyond traditional philanthropy by using market-based approaches to address social challenges.

### Key Principles

#### Intentionality
- **Purpose**: Explicit intention to create positive impact
- **Mission**: Clear social mission alignment
- **Strategy**: Impact-focused investment strategy
- **Measurement**: Systematic impact measurement
- **Reporting**: Transparent impact reporting

#### Additionality
- **Market Gap**: Filling gaps in traditional markets
- **Capital**: Providing capital where markets fail
- **Innovation**: Supporting innovative solutions
- **Scale**: Scaling proven solutions
- **Catalyst**: Acting as catalyst for change

#### Financial Return Expectations
- **Market**: Market-rate returns expected
- **Risk**: Appropriate risk-adjusted returns
- **Sustainable**: Financially sustainable models
- **Accountability**: Accountability for returns
- **Balance**: Balance between impact and returns

### Impact vs Traditional Investing

| Aspect | Traditional Investing | Social Impact Investing |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Objective** | Financial returns only | Financial + social returns |
| **Measurement** | Financial metrics only | Financial + impact metrics |
| **Time Horizon** | Short to medium term | Long-term impact focus |
| **Stakeholders** | Shareholders only | Multiple stakeholders |
| **Risk** | Financial risk only | Financial + impact risk |
| **Accountability** | Shareholder accountability | Stakeholder accountability |
