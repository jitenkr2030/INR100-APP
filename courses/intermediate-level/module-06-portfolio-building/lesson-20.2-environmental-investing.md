# Lesson 20.2: Environmental Investing

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Understand environmental investing themes and opportunities
- Analyze clean energy and climate technology investments
- Evaluate environmental impact measurement methodologies
- Compare green bonds and environmental investment products
- Build environmental-focused investment portfolios

## Environmental Investing Fundamentals

### What is Environmental Investing?
Environmental investing focuses on companies and projects that contribute to environmental sustainability, including renewable energy, clean technology, pollution control, resource efficiency, and climate change mitigation. It represents a growing investment theme with both financial and environmental returns.

### Key Environmental Themes

#### Clean Energy
- **Solar Power**: Photovoltaic and concentrated solar power
- **Wind Energy**: Onshore and offshore wind power
- **Hydroelectric**: Large and small-scale hydro power
- **Geothermal**: Geothermal energy systems
- **Nuclear**: Nuclear power (debated environmental benefits)

#### Energy Storage
- **Battery Technology**: Lithium-ion and emerging battery tech
- **Grid Storage**: Large-scale energy storage systems
- **Pumped Hydro**: Pumped hydroelectric storage
- **Hydrogen**: Green hydrogen production and storage
- **Thermal**: Thermal energy storage systems

#### Transportation
- **Electric Vehicles**: Battery electric vehicles (BEVs)
- **Charging Infrastructure**: EV charging stations
- **Public Transport**: Electric buses and trains
- **Sustainable Aviation**: Electric and hydrogen aircraft
- **Maritime**: Electric and hybrid shipping

#### Waste Management
- **Recycling**: Advanced recycling technologies
- **Waste-to-Energy**: Converting waste to energy
- **Circular Economy**: Resource recovery and reuse
- **Biodegradable**: Biodegradable materials
- **Ocean Cleanup**: Marine pollution solutions
