# Lesson 19.1: Introduction to Global Markets

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Understand major global markets and their characteristics
- Analyze economic indicators and market correlations
- Evaluate opportunities in international markets
- Compare domestic vs international investment benefits
- Assess currency and geopolitical risks

## Global Market Landscape

### Major Global Markets

#### United States Markets
- **NYSE & NASDAQ**: World's largest stock exchanges
- **Market Cap**: ~$50+ trillion total market capitalization
- **Key Indices**: S&P 500, NASDAQ Composite, Dow Jones
- **Trading Hours**: 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM EST
- **Currency**: US Dollar (USD)

**Market Characteristics:**
- **Liquidity**: Highest liquidity globally
- **Volatility**: Moderate to high volatility
- **Innovation**: Leading in technology and innovation
- **Regulation**: Well-regulated with strong investor protection
- **Access**: Easy access for international investors

#### European Markets
- **Major Exchanges**: London (LSE), Frankfurt (DAX), Paris (CAC)
- **Market Cap**: ~$20+ trillion combined
- **Key Indices**: FTSE 100, DAX, CAC 40, Euro Stoxx 50
- **Currency**: Euro, British Pound, Swiss Franc
- **Trading Hours**: 8:00 AM - 4:30 PM GMT

**Market Characteristics:**
- **Regulation**: MiFID II regulations
- **Sectors**: Strong in luxury goods, automotive, finance
- **Integration**: High market integration across EU
- **Stability**: Generally stable, lower volatility than US
- **Currency Risk**: Multiple currency exposure

#### Asian Markets
- **Major Exchanges**: Tokyo (Nikkei), Hong Kong (Hang Seng), Shanghai
- **Market Cap**: ~$30+ trillion combined
- **Key Indices**: Nikkei 225, Hang Seng, Shanghai Composite
- **Currency**: Japanese Yen, Hong Kong Dollar, Chinese Yuan
- **Trading Hours**: 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM local time

**Market Characteristics:**
- **Growth**: High growth potential
- **Volatility**: Higher volatility, especially emerging markets
- **Sectors**: Strong in technology, manufacturing, resources
- **Time Zones**: Different time zones provide global coverage
- **Regulation**: Varying regulatory frameworks

### Emerging Markets Overview

#### BRICS Nations
- **Brazil**: Resource-rich, agricultural powerhouse
- **Russia**: Energy and commodity exporter
- **India**: Fast-growing service economy
- **China**: World's second-largest economy
- **South Africa**: Gateway to African markets

#### ASEAN Markets
- **Singapore**: Financial hub and gateway
- **Malaysia**: Resource and manufacturing base

- **Indonesia**: Large consumer market
- **Philippines**: Service economy and BPO hub

#### Latin American Markets
- **Mexico**: NAFTA/USMCA beneficiary
- **Chile**: Stable, resource-rich economy
- **Colombia**: Growing consumer market
- **Argentina**: Commodity exporter
- **Brazil**: Largest Latin American economy

## Global Economic Indicators

### Key Economic Metrics

#### GDP Growth Rates
- **United States**: 2-3% typical growth
- **Germany**: 1-2% typical growth
- **Japan**: 1% typical growth
- **China**: 6-7% typical growth
- **India**: 6-8% typical growth

#### Inflation Rates
- **US Target**: 2% Federal Reserve target
- **EU Target**: 2% ECB target
- **Japan**: Historically below target
- **Emerging Markets**: 3-6% typical range
- **Impact**: Affects central bank policy and markets

#### Interest Rates
- **Federal Funds Rate**: US benchmark rate
- **ECB Rate**: European Central Bank rate
- **Bank Rate**: Bank of England rate
- **Repo Rate**: RBI rate for India
- **Global Impact**: Affects currency values and capital flows

### Market Correlation Analysis

#### Global Market Correlations
- **US-Europe**: High correlation (0.7-0.8)
- **US-Asia**: Moderate correlation (0.5-0.7)
- **Europe-Asia**: Lower correlation (0.3-0.5)
- **Emerging Markets**: Variable correlations
- **Benefit**: Diversification across uncorrelated markets

#### Sector Correlations
- **Technology**: High global correlation
- **Financials**: Moderate correlation
- **Consumer**: Varies by market maturity
- **Energy**: High commodity price correlation
- **Utilities**: Lower correlation, defensive sector

## Benefits of International Investing

### Diversification Benefits

#### Geographic Diversification
- **Risk Reduction**: Reduces concentration risk
- **Cycle Offset**: Different economic cycles
- **Currency Benefits**: Multiple currency exposure
- **Regulatory**: Different regulatory environments
- **Political**: Diversified political risk

#### Sector Diversification
- **Global Sectors**: Access to global leaders
- **Unique Sectors**: Some sectors more developed abroad
- **Innovation**: Access to global innovation
- **Growth**: High-growth sectors in emerging markets
- **Value**: Undervalued sectors in mature markets

### Return Enhancement

#### Historical Performance
- **Diversification**: Improved risk-adjusted returns
- **Growth**: Access to faster-growing economies
- **Innovation**: Technology and innovation exposure
- **Valuation**: Better valuation opportunities
- **Timing**: Access to different market cycles

#### Portfolio Benefits
- **Efficient Frontier**: Better efficient frontier
- **Sharpe Ratio**: Higher risk-adjusted returns
- **Maximum Drawdown**: Reduced maximum drawdowns
- **Volatility**: Smoother return patterns
- **Long-term**: Superior long-term performance

## Risks in International Investing

### Currency Risk

#### Exchange Rate Fluctuations
- **Translation Risk**: Currency impact on returns
- **Transaction Risk**: Currency conversion costs
- **Economic Risk**: Long-term currency trends
- **Political Risk**: Government intervention
- **Hedging**: Currency hedging strategies

#### Currency Impact Examples
- **USD Strength**: Negative for non-US holdings
- **USD Weakness**: Positive for non-US holdings
- **Emerging Currencies**: Higher volatility
- **Safe Havens**: CHF, JPY, USD during crises
- **Commodity Currencies**: AUD, CAD, NOK

### Political and Regulatory Risk

#### Political Instability
- **Government Changes**: Policy uncertainty
- **Elections**: Electoral volatility
- **Policy Risk**: Regulatory changes
- **Sanctions**: International sanctions
- **Nationalization**: Risk of asset seizure

#### Regulatory Differences
- **Reporting**: Different disclosure requirements
- **Taxation**: Varying tax treatments
- **Trading**: Different trading mechanisms
- **Liquidity**: Varying liquidity levels
- **Access**: Investment restrictions

### Market Structure Risk

#### Liquidity Risk
- **Trading Hours**: Limited overlap with domestic markets
- **Volume**: Lower trading volumes in some markets
- **Bid-Ask**: Wider spreads in less liquid markets
- **Size**: Difficulty in executing large orders
- **Timing**: Execution timing challenges

#### Information Risk
- **Language**: Language barriers
- **Time Zones**: Limited overlapping hours
- **Research**: Limited coverage by domestic analysts
- **Data**: Quality and availability of data
- **Transparency**: Varying transparency levels

## Global Investment Strategies

### Core-Satellite Approach

#### Core Holdings (70-80%)
- **Broad Market**: Global index funds/ETFs
- **Developed Markets**: US, Europe, Japan
- **Low Cost**: Expense ratio focus
- **Passive**: Passive management
- **Diversification**: Maximum diversification

#### Satellite Holdings (20-30%)
- **Themes**: Thematic investments
- **Regions**: Specific regions or countries
- **Sectors**: Sector-specific exposure
- **Active**: Active management
- **Opportunistic**: Opportunistic investments

### Regional Allocation Strategy

#### Developed Markets (60-70%)
- **United States**: 30-40% allocation
- **Europe**: 15-20% allocation
- **Japan**: 5-10% allocation
- **Other Developed**: 5-10% allocation
- **Benefits**: Stability, liquidity, growth

#### Emerging Markets (30-40%)
- **Asia**: 15-20% allocation
- **Latin America**: 5-10% allocation
- **Europe**: 5-10% allocation
- **Africa**: 2-5% allocation
- **Benefits**: Growth potential, diversification

### Currency-Hedged Strategy

#### Hedged Approach
- **Currency Neutral**: Remove currency risk
- **Hedge Ratio**: Partial or full hedging
- **Cost**: Hedging costs and benefits
- **Timing**: When to hedge
- **Flexibility**: Adjustable hedge ratios

#### Unhedged Approach
- **Currency Exposure**: Benefit from currency moves
- **Lower Cost**: No hedging costs
- **Volatility**: Higher return volatility
- **Timing**: Currency timing benefits
- **Diversification**: Currency diversification
