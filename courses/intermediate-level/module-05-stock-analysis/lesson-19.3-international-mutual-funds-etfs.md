## International Fund Fundamentals

### What are International Funds?
International mutual funds and ETFs invest in securities of companies located outside the investor's home country. They provide exposure to global markets through professionally managed portfolios, offering diversification benefits and access to international investment opportunities.

### Fund Structures

#### Open-End Funds (Mutual Funds)
- **Continuous**: Continuous offering and redemption
- **NAV**: Net Asset Value pricing daily
- **Liquidity**: Daily liquidity at NAV
- **Management**: Active portfolio management
- **Expense**: Management fees and expenses

#### Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)
- **Trading**: Trade on stock exchanges like stocks
- **Intraday**: Intraday trading capabilities
- **Creation**: Creation/redemption mechanism
- **Tracking**: Typically track indexes
- **Cost**: Generally lower expenses than mutual funds

### Geographic Classifications

#### Global Funds
- **Worldwide**: Invest globally including home country
- **Diversification**: Maximum diversification
- **Currency**: Multiple currency exposure
- **Management**: Global asset allocation decisions
- **Examples**: Vanguard Total World Stock ETF (VT)

#### International Funds
- **Foreign Only**: Exclude home country investments
- **Diversification**: International diversification only
- **Currency**: Foreign currency exposure
- **Regional**: May focus on specific regions
- **Examples**: iShares MSCI ACWI ex-US ETF (ACWX)

#### Regional Funds
- **Specific Regions**: Focus on specific regions
- **European**: European equity funds
- **Asian**: Asian equity funds
- **Emerging**: Emerging market funds
- **Developed**: Developed market funds
