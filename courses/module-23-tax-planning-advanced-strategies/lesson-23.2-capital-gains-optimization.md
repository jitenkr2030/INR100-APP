## Capital Gains Fundamentals

### What are Capital Gains?
Capital gains are profits realized when you sell an investment for more than your original purchase price (cost basis). They represent the appreciation in value of an investment and are subject to taxation when realized.

### Key Concepts

#### Cost Basis
- **Purchase Price**: Original purchase price
- **Transaction Costs**: Buying and selling commissions
- **Improvements**: Capital improvements to property
- **Adjustments**: Basis adjustments over time
- **Documentation**: Maintaining accurate records

#### Holding Period
- **Short-term**: 1 year or less holding period
- **Long-term**: More than 1 year holding period
- **Calculation**: From acquisition to disposition date
- **Tax Treatment**: Different tax rates apply
- **Planning**: Strategic holding period management

### Capital Gains vs Ordinary Income

| Aspect | Capital Gains | Ordinary Income |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Tax Rate** | Preferential rates | Regular income rates |
| **Holding Period** | Long-term vs short-term | No holding period |
| **Timing** | Realization-based | Recognition-based |
| **Losses** | Capital losses offset gains | No offset with income |
| **Planning** | Strategic timing opportunities | Limited planning options |

## Short-term vs Long-term Capital Gains

### Short-term Capital Gains

#### Definition and Tax Treatment
- **Holding Period**: 1 year or less
- **Tax Rate**: Taxed as ordinary income
- **Higher Rate**: Higher tax rates for most taxpayers
- **Planning**: Limited tax planning opportunities
- **Strategy**: Avoid short-term gains when possible

#### Examples
- **Stock Trading**: Stocks held for less than 1 year
- **Mutual Funds**: Mutual fund redemptions within 1 year
- **Real Estate**: Real estate held for less than 1 year
- **Bonds**: Bonds held for less than 1 year
- **Collectibles**: Collectibles held for less than 1 year

### Long-term Capital Gains

#### Definition and Tax Treatment
- **Holding Period**: More than 1 year
- **Tax Rates**: Preferential tax rates (0%, 15%, 20%)
- **Lower Rate**: Significantly lower than ordinary income
- **Planning**: Significant tax planning opportunities
- **Strategy**: Hold investments for long-term benefits

#### Current Tax Rates (India)
- **Up to ₹2.5 lakh**: 0% (no tax)
- **₹2.5-5 lakh**: 5% on gains above ₹2.5 lakh
- **₹5-10 lakh**: 10% on gains above ₹5 lakh
- **Above ₹10 lakh**: 15% on gains above ₹10 lakh
- **Surcharge**: Additional surcharge applicable

#### Examples
- **Stocks**: Stocks held for more than 1 year
- **Mutual Funds**: ELSS funds held for more than 1 year
- **Real Estate**: Property held for more than 1 year
- **Bonds**: Long-term bond holdings
- **Gold**: Gold held for more than 1 year

## Tax-Efficient Selling Strategies

### Lot Selection Methods

#### FIFO (First In, First Out)
- **Method**: Sell oldest lots first
- **Simple**: Simplest method to implement
- **IRS**: Default method if not specified
- **Documentation**: Requires good records
- **Planning**: Limited planning opportunities

#### LIFO (Last In, First Out)
- **Method**: Sell newest lots first
- **Tax**: Can minimize taxes in rising markets
- **Documentation**: Requires detailed records
- **Planning**: Better tax planning opportunities
- **Compliance**: Must specify method

#### Specific Identification
- **Method**: Identify specific lots to sell
- **Tax**: Maximum tax planning flexibility
- **Documentation**: Requires excellent records
- **Planning**: Optimal tax planning
- **Requirements**: Specific identification ability

#### Average Cost Method
- **Method**: Use average cost for identical securities
- **Simple**: Simple calculation method
- **Limited**: Limited to specific securities
- **Planning**: Moderate planning opportunities
- **Mutual**: Often used for mutual funds
